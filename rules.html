Rules
Everything you need to know before you race

Overview

Monday Military Madness is a community Zwift race series built around one featured race each month. The aim is to create competitive, welcoming and enjoyable racing across a full campaign season.

Each round forms part of the wider series, with riders able to compete for overall standings, GC, sprint and K/QOM honours across the campaign.

Event Format

Each round has its own published race card showing the official event details, including type, laps, distance, elevation, sprint details and K/QOM details.

Riders should check the race card before each event so they understand the format and what is being contested.

Scoring & Classifications

Monday Military Madness tracks several classifications across the campaign.

Overall is based on points scored from race finishes.
GC is based on accumulated race time across the series.
Sprint tracks points scored at designated sprint segments.
K/QOM tracks points scored at designated climbing segments.
Category Leaders recognise the leading riders within each Zwift category.

Standings are updated after each race and published on the site.

Categories & Pens

Riders are responsible for entering the correct category or pen for each event.

Where ZRS pens are used, riders should join the pen that matches their Zwift Racing Score. Riders should always follow the event setup shown on the race card.

Results

Official results are published after each race. These may include finishing position, finish time, finish points, sprint points, K/QOM points, total points and updated standings.

The published standings are the official record of the series.

Fair Play

Monday Military Madness is intended to be raced hard, fairly and in good spirit.

  • Enter the correct category or pen
  • Use accurate rider data
  • Compete fairly
  • Respect fellow riders and organisers

Any behaviour judged to undermine fair competition may be reviewed.

Organiser Decisions

The organiser reserves the right to review and amend results, points or classifications where required to protect fairness and the integrity of the series.

Where a decision is needed, the organiser’s ruling will be final.
